WHAT IS THE MAIN EFFECT OF CLIMATE CHANGE?


In short: The air is getting too warm.

EFFECTS OF CLIMATE CHANGE ON HUMAN HEALTH


Plants are exposed to warmer temperatures as a result of increased CO2
concentration in the earth's atmosphere. This causes them to grow more rapidly and
produce more pollen, which worsens allergies among the
human population. Climate change also increases ground ozone levelsOzone is a trioxygen molecule found in the earth's stratosphere where they absorb ultraviolet rays from solar radiation that can be harmful to life; it is also found on the earth's surface, formed from the combination of human activity and the presence of sunlight.
and fine particle concentrations, which can trigger more chest pains, coughing,
throat irritation, and cause inflammation of the lungs.

EFFECTS OF CLIMATE CHANGE ON THE ENVIRONMENT


As the atmosphere warms, glaciers and polar ice sheets melt rapidly, According to satelite data, the earth loses 287 gigatonnes (287 billion tons) of land ice annually.
causing a sudden rise in sea level. this rise has the tendency
to both contaminate our drinking water and interfere with farming.
Weather patterns become more extreme, as frequent heatwaves increase
the evaporation of water, fueling severe storms. In more dry areas, climate change
intensifies droughts and wildfires.
